About Myself

Pritpal Singh received the Ph.D. degree in computer science and engineering from Tezpur (Central) University, Tezpur, India, in February 2015. He received a Master Degree in Computer Applications from Dibrugarh University, Assam (India), in 2008. From July 2009 to June 2013, he was a Senior Research Fellow at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Tezpur (Central) University. In July 2013, he was appointed as a Lecturer at the School of Mathematics and Computer Applications, Thapar University, Punjab (India). From November 2015 to February 2019, he worked as an Assistant Professor at the Faculty of Computer Applications (FCA), CHARUSAT Campus, Anand, Gujarat (India).

He has been appointed as a Faculty with the School of Mathematics and Computer Applications, Thapar University, Patiala, India, in July 2013. He worked as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow with the Department of Electrical Engineering, National Taipei University of Technology, New Taipei, Taiwan, and as an Adjunct Professor (Research) with the Institute of Theoretical Physics, Jagiellonian University, Poland. He is currently an Assistant Professor with the Department of Data Science and Analytics, Central University of Rajasthan, Ajmer, India.

He has published numerous papers in refereed SCI journals, conference proceedings, book chapters, and books. His research articles can be found in IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man and Cybernetics: Systems, Information Sciences (Elsevier), Artificial Intelligence in Medicine (Elsevier), Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine (Elsevier), Knowledge-Based Systems (Elsevier), International Journal of Approximate Reasoning (Elsevier), Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence (Elsevier), Applied Soft Computing (Elsevier), Journal of Computational Science (Elsevier), Computers in Industry (Elsevier), Expert Systems With Applications (Elsevier), among others.

His research interests include ambiguous set theory, optimization algorithms (especially quantum-based optimization), time series forecasting, image analysis, fMRI data analysis, machine learning, and mathematical modeling and simulation.

Dr. Singh has been awarded a Postdoctoral Research Fellowship from the Ministry of Science and Technology, Taiwan, in March 2019. He also received the prestigious International Visiting Research Fellowship from the Foundation for Polish Science, Poland, in 2020.

Dr. Singh's name has been continuously listed among the world's top 2% of scientists in 2023 and 2024.

Quantum Research lab

Quantum Computation & Ambiguous Set Lab (QCASL)

Quantum Computation & Ambiguous Set Lab (QCASL) is a pioneering research hub dedicated to advancing knowledge and applications in two cutting-edge fields: quantum computation and ambiguous set theory. Situated at the intersection of theoretical research and practical innovation, QCASL is driven by the mission to explore new mathematical frameworks, computational techniques, and algorithms that address complex problems in uncertain environments and quantum domains.

Research Focus Areas

1. Quantum Optimization and Computation

QCASL focuses on the development of optimization algorithms that leverage the principles of quantum mechanics. Our work involves designing and analyzing quantum algorithms for solving complex optimization problems that are often computationally intensive for classical systems. This includes areas like quantum annealing, variational quantum algorithms, and quantum-inspired optimization for large-scale applications in fields such as cryptography, logistics, and artificial intelligence.

2. Ambiguous Set Theory and Fuzzy Logic

Ambiguous set theory, an innovative extension of classical set theory, serves as a foundation for handling situations where uncertainty and partial truth values exist. QCASL investigates mathematical models and frameworks that incorporate ambiguity, refining theoretical foundations and exploring applications in areas where binary classifications are insufficient. This research has potential applications in fields such as decision science, artificial intelligence, and data-driven systems that require nuanced handling of incomplete or vague information.

3. Quantum-Inspired Metaheuristics and Optimization Techniques

Beyond quantum hardware, QCASL also works on quantum-inspired algorithms and metaheuristics for optimization. By borrowing concepts from quantum mechanics, such as superposition and entanglement, the lab develops novel methods that enhance classical algorithms’ performance, making them more efficient in solving combinatorial and continuous optimization problems under ambiguity.

4. Hybrid Quantum-Ambiguous Systems

A unique aspect of QCASL's work is the integration of quantum computation principles with ambiguous set theory. This hybrid approach aims to address the inherent uncertainties in quantum systems, potentially improving quantum algorithm reliability and adaptability. This research area investigates how ambiguous sets and quantum states can be co-optimized, leading to breakthroughs in applications such as quantum machine learning, uncertain data processing, and probabilistic reasoning.

Collaborative and Educational Initiatives

As part of its educational outreach, QCASL provides training opportunities for students, researchers, and industry professionals interested in quantum computation and ambiguous set theory. The lab frequently collaborates with academic institutions, government agencies, and industry partners to drive innovation and foster knowledge-sharing.

Quantum Computation & Ambiguous Set Lab (QCASL) stands at the forefront of interdisciplinary research, addressing some of the most complex and evolving challenges in modern computation and uncertainty. Through innovative approaches and a commitment to theoretical and applied research, QCASL continues to make significant contributions to the fields of quantum computation and ambiguous set theory,preparing the next generation of researchers for the challenges of tomorrow.
